practicalSteps:
  - Watch how the fish swim, breathe, and rest.
  - Check whether the fish is hiding more than usual or staying near the surface.
  - Compare behavior to the species' normal habits on the care sheet.
  - Check the water before assuming the fish is the only problem.

Fish rarely say they are stressed in a single obvious way. More often the warning
signs show up as small changes in swimming, breathing, hiding, or feeding.

The useful habit is to compare what you see with the fish's normal behavior. If
the water is off, or the fish is acting unlike itself, treat that as a cue to slow
down and check the tank before making other changes.
