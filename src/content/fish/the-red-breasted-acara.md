commonName: The Red Breasted Acara
scientificName: Laetacara dorsigera
origin: Brazil, Bolivia, Paraguay, and Argentina
temperatureRange: 76 - 82 F
adultSize: 2 to  2.5 inches
tankSize: 20 gallon or larger
foodRecommendation: "Pelleted foods, frozen, and live "
difficulty: Intermediate
temperament: "This species is very peaceful to other fish and can be safely kept
  with all coryodorus and all but the smallest tetras.   "
plantSafe: true
invertebrateSafe: false
juvenileAppearance: Juveniles are silver in appearance with brown lines that form on their flanks.
adultMaleAppearance: Males become intensly red especially during
  spawning.  These colors become very vivid as eggs are laid.
adultFemaleAppearance: Similar to the appearance of the male but not as intensely colored.
husbandryNotes: Laetacara dorsigera is a very challenging species to keep alive
  through the juvenile phase.  It is highly recommended that the hobbyist
  watches the progress of the juveniles very carefully.   The sheer quantity of
  off spring and the amount that each individual seems to need may exceed the
  resources of the tank that they are housed in.  Additionally the parents are
  fiercely protective of the offspring so much so that they will readily corral
  them to places where there is not food if they perceive a threat.  Thusly if
  the fry show signs of losing weight it is highly recommended that they be
  moved into separate breeding boxes In order to keep from experiencing massive
  losses.   The newly hatched fry readily take paramecium aurellia but after a
  few days can be switch to brine shrimp naupli.
quickWarnings:
  - Egg layer, eggs are desposited on cleaned sections of wood.
