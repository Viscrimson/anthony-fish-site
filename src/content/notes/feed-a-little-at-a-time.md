quickAnswer: Start with a small amount, watch how the fish responds, and add more only if the food is gone quickly.
practicalSteps:
  - Offer a small portion rather than filling the tank with food.
  - Watch whether the fish eat right away or ignore the food.
  - Remove leftovers before they sink and break down.
  - Adjust the amount only after you have seen a few feedings in a row.

Most aquarium fish do better with smaller feedings than with big, messy meals.
Food that disappears quickly is usually easier on the tank and easier on the fish.

The goal is to feed enough for the fish to eat, not enough to leave extra debris.
If the fish seem hungry, feed a little more next time instead of doubling the amount at
once.
