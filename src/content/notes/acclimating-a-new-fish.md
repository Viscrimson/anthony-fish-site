Acclimation is the part that helps a fish move from one water environment to
another without a sudden shock. The exact method can vary, but the idea stays
the same: keep the changes slow enough that the fish has time to adjust.

For a home aquarium, temperature is usually the first thing to calm down. After
that, a gentle transition into tank water is often enough for a simple setup.
The details matter less than keeping the process calm, deliberate, and free of
extra stress.

Once the fish is in the aquarium, the next step is observation. The first day
note covers what to watch after the fish has settled in the tank.
