The fish in our care originally came from wild stock.  They have evolved for the conditions in these wilderness areas.   Many fish as such are exposed to nutrients that come from a variety of sources, bugs, crustaceans, phytoplankton, plant material, and even living and dead fish comprise the diets of many wild fish.  In an aquarium environment it is wise do first do your research, it is very useful to select foods based upon the feeding habits of the fish in the wild.  Studies here can be useful in determining what foods to target and what foods to feed.  Next determine how much food is required to not only feed the top water and mid water swimmers but remember the bottom dwellers must also receive food.  This will mean at least at the beginning you will be feeding fish more then they can immediately consume.  What constitutes the right amount of food can be very difficult at least initially to determine and as such you need to build in some redundancy into your tanks, include scavengers that can eat up the excess food, break it down, and enough plants to take the waste material and incorporate it into their tissues.
