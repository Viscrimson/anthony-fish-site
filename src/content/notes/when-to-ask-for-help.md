There are four methods in which to acquire stock for your aquarium.  It is important to note that each method has its own strengths and its own weaknesses and none are inherently superior to the other.  We will examine the strengths and the drawbacks now of each methodology and please feel free to consult related pages if you need recommendations on how to overcome some of the drawbacks of each method:

1.)   Local Fish Store:

-  The Benefit:   Local fish stores commonly contain the widest variety of fish available to the hobbyist.  It is commonly an amalgamation of multiple sources.  From fish farms, to wholesalers both foreign and domestic, and finally hobbyist breeders local fish stores have the ability to draw from an immense variety of sources and for that reason alone they represent the best chance the hobbyist has of finding a particular fish that they have selected for their tank.   They also commonly possess specialized knowledge in that species and you can commonly find that local fish stores are owned and operated by hobbyists themselves who have prior to owning a store gained specialized knowledge that allows them to provide a unique amount of advice on a species and to be a valuable source of knowledge that the hobbyist can consult in order to make more educated decisions on how to overcome particular challenges.  The final benefit of a local fish store that can't be overstated is that the hobbyist can choose which particular animal they want.  You may of course want to select this for color patterns that often indicate the sex of the fish or you may want to select based on behavior.  It is very common for a LFS to show proof that the animal is eating before many hobbyists will purchase it.
- The Drawback:     The first drawback that you have to overcome as a hobbyist is that the store owner themselves have to price in the cost of a brick and mortar building into the price of the fish that you are going to purchase.  Additionally the tanks themselves are often times a vector of disease.  What you have to understand is that each time a local fish store purchases new stock, that stock may be arriving carrying disease itself.  As such the fish may very well be sick and the pathogens that cause those illnesses may be infesting the tank and the filtration itself spreading illness amongst the inhabitants of that tank.  Due to the fact that many LFS operate on smaller margins, many do not cycle through tanks in order to cleanse them thus pathogens begin to build up and fish that look fine when purchased can come down with illnesses one or two weeks after arriving in the home of a hobbyist.  Please consult the resource on hospital tanks in order to learn how to address this drawback and to keep the problems confined and to help eliminate the effects of disease from the aquariums you wish to display.

2.)  Online Store:

- The Benefit:     Online stores often times focus upon particular species or suppliers.  You can think of them as being very similar to an LFS only with online stores they select the stock you tell them that you want and then they ship the fish to you.  Online stores often times have a much wider variety of rarer stock then local fish stores do.   The reason for this is that a local fish store tends to be more geared toward a customer that walks into the door.  Thus you will find local fish stores flush with commonly requested fish such as cardinal tetras, angelfish and various livebearers such as guppies, swordtails, and platys.   What an online store does is serve customers who aren't interested in the common fare.  They serve a clientele that is looking for a specific species, to put together a specific biotope, or the hobbyist which has more specialized goals in mind.  A brick and mortar store normally doesn't have enough demand to warrant devoting space to the fish that this subdivision of the hobby is more interested in and thus the online store steps in to fill the need.   Additionally you have stores such as Dan's Fish, Dave's Rare Aquarium Fish, and Uncle Joe's Fish Shed that are more geared toward acquisition from hobbyists.  This helps to ensure that they quality of the stock that is attained is significantly higher.  Each shop has its own additional internal controls that directly translate into healthier fish being shipped to the customer.  Additionally these stores are commonly expert shippers, having DOA fish or even struggling fish means that customers would often question or be less likely to choose an online shipper to acquire new specimens.  For this reason every online store has a vested interest in making certain that the fish that arrive have the best start possible.   This often translates to elite care such as vet services on the stock, internal testing, expert packing, and a reliance on procedure that minimize the chance of animals being shipped in foul water.
- The Drawback:      The online store is hands down the most expensive of the choices available to the hobbyist.  In addition to an often larger expense when acquiring individual fish, you will also have to deal with a very expensive shipping procedure and you will be at the mercy of the carrier to deliver your fish to you at a specific time.  It can't be understated that shipping delays, the reliability of the carrier, and the expense alone are enough to cause many hobbyists to seek another means of acquiring fish.   The burden of taking off of work is often one that many hobbyists do not have the option to do and if your carrier leaves the box in the open sun during a Florida summer for instance the hobbyist will likely open the box coming home from work only to find dead fish carefully packed inside.

3.)  Club Auction

-  The Benefit:  When you purchase from hobbyists directly you are often purchasing a higher quality of stock, that is cheaper then you can get anywhere else and has the additional benefit of having a name and a face behind the sale with expert knowledge of how the fish was kept, how it was fed, and how it was handled prior to arrival into your home.   It isn't an exaggeration to say that acquiring fish in this manner can be the very cheapest way that can't be matched by any store front.   Often times many fish can be purchased at a club auction for a fraction of the amount of the price of one.  As many hobbyists take personal pride in what they raise and consider themselves in many cases as people on a mission to lessen the affects of wild collection on the environment you can often times find a higher quality fish from hobbyists at club auctions.  You may in some cases also find that hobbyists are actively developing strains of fish that can be found no where else.   This can result in fish of a given species baring unique markings or displaying unique traits that make them markedly different then wild varieties.
- The Drawback:   There are many drawbacks from purchasing fish at a club auction.   The first is that there is a very low chance of a particular fish being made available at any one auction.  Often times what is available is a very low variety that represents only what the particular hobbyist was interested in at that particular time.   Additionally hobbyists are experts in husbandry, they aren't exactly experts at preparing fish to be sold nor do they provide a warranty in case fish are lost.   This means that you often find fish in bags that are woefully underprepared for sale, that have not been prepped for a long period of time in a bag and thus are often subject to ammonia burn, temperature shock, and low oxygen while in those bags.  Please consult the related resource on how to handle club auctions, transportation, and acclimation if you need help dealing with these types of problems.
