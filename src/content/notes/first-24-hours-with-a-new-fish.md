The first day is mostly about making the tank feel predictable. A fish that has
just been moved does better when the aquarium stays quiet and there is enough
time to settle in without extra stress.

If the fish is swimming normally and exploring the tank, that is usually a good
sign that the handoff went well. It is still worth watching closely for a while
because a new environment can show problems that were not obvious in the bag or
transport container.

This note covers the first day only. For the handoff itself, see the
acclimation guide. For checking the tank, see the basic water testing note.
