quickAnswer: Use enough flow to keep the tank moving, but not so much that shy or slow fish struggle against it.
practicalSteps:
  - Check whether the species in the tank prefer calmer or stronger movement.
  - Watch if fish are being pushed around the tank or avoiding open water.
  - Add plants, hardscape, or filter adjustments if the current is too strong.
  - Recheck after stocking changes because the right flow can change with the mix of fish.
visualNote: A simple low-flow / medium-flow / strong-flow scale is easier to read than technical jargon.

The right filter flow is the flow the fish can actually live with. A tank can be
clean and still be uncomfortable if the current pushes every fish around.

Watch how the fish move through the water, not just how the filter looks on paper.
If the fish spend all their time fighting the current or hiding from it, the setup
needs a calmer path.
