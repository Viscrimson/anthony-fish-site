husbandryNotes: >-
  These fish should be provided with sandy bottoms to which they can engage in
  their sand sifting behavior.     They will scoop up sand and expel the sand
  through their gills sifting it for organic pieces that they will
  consume.  Males will engage in a dance where they will open their mouths wide
  into an "O" shape and they will shake their heads vigorously back and forth
  both in a display of aggression and to impress the female fish.


  This fish is a female mouthbrooder and after breeding has occurred it is
  highly recommended that you wait three days before attempting to remove the
  female from the tank.  Females should be removed from the tank otherwise you
  run the risk of the female not feeling secure enough to release the
  fry.  Females that do not feel secure will hold the fry to the point of
  killing them from a lack of sustenance if she isn't otherwise
  removed.   Waiting a period of three days also prevents the female from
  feeling compelled enough when chased to swallow he eggs.   


  Remove the egg carrying female to a 30 gallon long footprint tank that is
  planted.   When the female feels secure she will release the babies and
  oversee their care.   She will pick them back up if she feels insecure, this
  is very reminiscent of African cichlid behavior.  Once she starts to wander
  away from the babies it is time to remove her.  The babies once free swimming
  will actively chase down and consume newly hatched brine shrimp.   This
  species tends to grow in spurts and can take a long time to reach full
  maturity.
