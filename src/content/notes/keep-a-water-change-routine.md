quickAnswer: Do regular water changes on a schedule that matches the tank instead of waiting until it looks bad.
practicalSteps:
  - Pick a schedule you can actually keep.
  - Remove and replace a portion of the water at the same time each week or every other week.
  - Match the new water to the tank as closely as practical.
  - Test the water if the tank starts to look or smell different than usual.

Water changes work best when they are boring and regular. The routine matters more
than trying to do a huge cleanup only when something goes wrong.

A steady schedule helps keep waste and dissolved buildup under control. It also gives
you a repeatable point where you can test the tank, compare notes, and notice changes
before they become problems.
